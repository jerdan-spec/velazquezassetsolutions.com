What people ask us

Tax surplus recovery is new to most people. Here are the questions we hear most often.

What is tax surplus and how does it happen?
When a county forecloses on a property for unpaid taxes and sells it at auction, the sale sometimes generates more money than what was owed. That excess — called surplus or excess proceeds — legally belongs to the former property owner, not the county. Most people have no idea this money exists.
How much does your service cost?
Nothing upfront. We charge a 5% consulting fee, and only collect it if we successfully recover funds for you. If we don't find anything or the claim doesn't go through, you owe us nothing.
How long does the recovery process take?
It varies by county, but most claims in Washington State resolve within 30 to 90 days after filing. We'll give you a realistic timeline once we've reviewed your specific case.
What if I'm not sure whether I have a claim?
That's exactly what our free case review is for. Just call us with your property information and we'll research it at no charge. If there's money available, we'll tell you. If there isn't, we'll tell you that too.
Which counties do you cover?
We currently serve King, Pierce, Snohomish, Clark, Spokane, Thurston, Yakima, Whatcom, Kitsap, and Island counties — covering the majority of Washington State's tax foreclosure activity.
